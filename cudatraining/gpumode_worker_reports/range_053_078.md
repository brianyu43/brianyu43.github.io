- **Thin local descriptions for many lectures**: 054 and 059 lack raw video description files; 056/058/060/062/063/064/068/076/077 descriptions mostly name speakers only. These are best framed as topic previews, not deep recaps.
- **Slides/code coverage is uneven**: local `status_report.md` marks most of 053-078 as missing code, and many as missing slides. Stronger local backing exists for 057, 067, 069, 070, 071, 072, 074, 075, and 078.
- **ScaleML/GPT-OSS wording risk**: descriptions for 071-075 mention OpenAI's "frontier open-source model, GPT-OSS." Keep that as a description-derived event framing, not an independently expanded product claim.
- **Title-derived inference risk**: notes for many lectures explicitly say technical themes are unclear from available sources. Do not infer algorithm details from titles such as LeanRL, D-Matrix Corsair, FastVideo, Game Arena, BackendBench, or Iris without a slide/repo/manual-video check.
- **Naming consistency risk**: preserve official local metadata titles, including punctuation variants like `Lecture 75 [ScaleML Series]...` and `Lecture 78 Iris...`; avoid silently normalizing if generated pages later cross-link by title.
- **Korean design risk**: ep.42's voice is vivid and conversational, but for these pages the archive evidence is partial. Use the same warm editorial style while keeping evidence labels visible.
